# Project Signal Wizard — Product Purpose

## Product Thesis

Project Signal Wizard is a contractor-focused project-control assistant.

The initial MVP is the Document Control Section because controlled project documentation is the first and most important source of truth in engineering and construction projects. Once this base is established, the product expands into project signals, material control, QA/QC readiness, procurement support, subcontractor coordination, contract/VO awareness, and guided project execution.

The goal is not to build another generic project-management platform.

The goal is to help project managers and discipline leads detect earlier what experienced project managers learn to notice through years of painful field experience.

---

## Core Insight

New project managers naturally run to the field first.

That is normal and even necessary. They need to get dirty, understand the work, earn trust, and learn through direct exposure.

The problem is that by late afternoon, when the field calms down, most of the real project-management work begins: checking what happened, what did not happen, and what tomorrow's work is already at risk of failing.

Experienced project managers hear the field differently.

They do not only hear complaints. They hear signals.

Project Signal Wizard is designed to help less-experienced managers develop those ears earlier.

---

## Two Primary Truth Sources

### 1. Document Control

Document control tells the project what was designed, approved, submitted, reviewed, revised, rejected, reported, recorded, claimed, and contractually communicated.

A strong document-control system can expose:

- late engineering;
- wrong revisions;
- missing approvals;
- open comments;
- missing QA/QC evidence;
- unresolved RFIs/TQs;
- missing turnover records;
- undocumented changes;
- weak claim/VO evidence;
- superseded documents still in use.

### 2. Material / Warehouse Control

Material control tells the project what physically moved.

It can expose:

- missing material;
- wrong material issued to the field;
- material issued against the wrong drawing or revision;
- material available but not released;
- material installed without required documentation;
- mismatch between reported progress and actual material movement;
- procurement or logistics blockers;
- subcontractor execution inconsistencies.

The strongest project signals appear when document truth and material truth disagree.

---

## Product Direction

Project Signal Wizard should evolve from document control into a project signal mentor.

It should listen to the project, detect mismatches, raise short and useful warnings, and help the project manager decide where to look next.

Example signals:

- A crew took material that does not match the drawing they are working on.
- An isometric was released to fabrication but critical material is missing.
- A pipe spool is planned for lifting tomorrow but NDT reports are not submitted.
- A subcontractor is claiming progress that is not supported by material movement or QA/QC records.
- Work appears to continue under a change condition without an approved VO path.
- A document revision changed after fabrication or procurement activity already started.

## Role of AI

AI is not the product by itself.

AI is a capability layer inside a controlled project system.

### Suitable AI Uses

- Natural-language project search.
- Mobile project knowledge assistant.
- Meeting summaries.
- Daily and weekly project briefs.
- Report generation.
- Metadata extraction from documents.
- Drafting RFIs, TQs, RFQs, reminders, and internal updates.
- Summarizing supplier and subcontractor responses.
- Turning field noise into structured signals.
- Explaining why a signal matters.

### Suitable Automation Uses

- Repetitive report preparation.
- Draft RFQ preparation.
- Supplier follow-up workflows.
- BOM/MTO assistance.
- Document package readiness checks.
- Missing-record detection.
- Reminder and escalation workflows.

### Hard Boundary

The system may prepare, suggest, summarize, draft, compare, and warn.

For consequential external actions, commercial decisions, contractual notices, approvals, or production-data changes, a human must approve.

---

## Product Principle

For every workflow, ask:

> Does a human really need to do this?

Then ask:

> If not, is it safe and permitted for the system to do it without approval?

This creates the operating rule:

> Automate where safe. Require human approval where consequences exist.

---

## Initial MVP Interpretation

The first MVP is the document-control MVP.

However, it should be understood as the MVP for the document section of a broader project-control product.

It is not intended to compete directly with large EPC/client platforms such as Aconex, Autodesk Construction Cloud, Procore, or similar systems.

It is intended to build the first reliable project-data backbone for contractor-side execution intelligence.

## Primary Users

### Primary

- Project managers at contractors.
- Project engineers at contractors.
- Discipline managers at contractors.

### Secondary

- Discipline leads inside EPC organizations.
- Document controllers.
- Material controllers / warehouse teams.
- QA/QC managers.
- Procurement coordinators.

---

## Product Mission

Help the next generation of project managers avoid the avoidable chaos: unmanaged extras, missing approvals, wrong revisions, undocumented changes, late material, weak claims, quality surprises, turnover panic, and endless evening crisis management.

The product should not replace experience.

It should compress the learning curve.
